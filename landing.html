The Problem
"I knew how to build. I just didn't know what to build next."

Building a SaaS is not a straight line. It's a maze of decisions — each one made harder by the fact that nobody tells you the order things need to happen. You launch before validating. You validate before building your legal foundation. You scale before you've nailed retention.

Every founder reinvents the same playbook. Hours researching what phases exist. Weeks figuring out what order they go in. The hidden cost isn't time — it's the compounding mistakes from skipping what you didn't know to look for.

How it works

Simple by design.

01
Pick your stage

Open the tracker and navigate to where you are in your journey. Each of the 18 phases is laid out clearly — from ideation all the way to scaling.

02
Check off milestones

Work through each item at your own pace. Every milestone has a description explaining what it is and why it matters. Nothing is left unexplained.

03
Ship with confidence

Progress saves automatically. Come back whenever you're ready. No accounts, no subscriptions — just a clear view of where you've been and what's ahead.

What's inside

Built for founders.
Ready for developers.

Whether you're tracking your own SaaS journey or embedding it inside your product to help your users — everything you need is already built in.

💾
Auto-saved progress

Everything saves to localStorage automatically. Survives page refreshes, tab closes, and browser restarts without any backend.

🎨
Dark & Light theme

Toggle between dark and light with a single attribute. Shadow DOM keeps the component isolated from your site's styles completely.

⚡
Zero dependencies

One JavaScript file. No npm install. No React. No build pipeline. Just a script tag and you're done.

🗂️
Multi-project support

Use the project attribute to run multiple independent trackers on the same page for different SaaS ideas.

📡
Custom events

Hook into item-toggle and phase-complete to connect to your analytics, backend, or unlock features.

📱
Fully responsive

Works on every screen size. The sidebar collapses gracefully on mobile so nothing is ever hidden or broken.
